## Persona 1: The Concerned Parent

### Sarah Chen
**Age**: 42  
**Occupation**: Marketing Manager  
**Location**: Suburban area  
**Tech Savviness**: Medium  

#### Background
- Parent of a 10th-grade student struggling with AP Chemistry
- Works full-time, limited availability during weekday afternoons
- Active in parent-teacher organizations
- Values education highly, willing to invest in tutoring

#### Goals
- Find a qualified tutor who can improve her daughter's grades
- Ensure the tutor has relevant expertise (not just a general tutor)
- Flexible scheduling that works with family commitments
- Track progress and see measurable improvements

#### Pain Points
- Previous tutoring experiences were disappointing (tutor cancelled frequently)
- Concerned about cost vs. value
- Wants transparency: What will be covered? How will success be measured?
- Limited time to research and vet multiple tutors

#### Behaviors
- Reads reviews and testimonials carefully
- Asks detailed questions before committing
- Prefers email communication for scheduling
- Values professionalism and responsiveness

#### Quote
> "I need to know my daughter is in good hands and that we'll see real improvement, not just someone going through the motions."

#### How We Serve This Persona
- Detailed tutor qualifications and experience prominently displayed
- Clear testimonials from parents of similar students
- Transparent pricing and session structure
- Responsive communication (within 24 hours)
- Progress tracking and regular feedback

## Persona 2: The Motivated Student

### Alex Rodriguez
**Age**: 17  
**Grade**: 11th (Junior)  
**Location**: Urban area  
**Tech Savviness**: High  

#### Background
- Strong student aiming for top-tier universities
- Taking multiple AP courses
- Self-directed learner, but wants expert guidance for SAT/ACT prep
- Active in extracurriculars (debate team, volunteer work)

#### Goals
- Maximize SAT score for college applications
- Learn test-taking strategies from an expert
- Efficient sessions that respect his busy schedule
- Access to practice materials and resources between sessions

#### Pain Points
- Extremely busy schedule (school, extracurriculars, part-time job)
- Frustrated by generic test prep classes
- Wants personalized attention, not one-size-fits-all
- Needs flexibility to reschedule when conflicts arise

#### Behaviors
- Researches independently (reads blog posts, watches YouTube videos)
- Prefers online sessions over in-person (saves commute time)
- Uses mobile device frequently for scheduling and communication
- Self-motivated, but appreciates accountability

#### Quote
> "I don't need someone to reteach me everythingâ€”I need strategic help on the specific areas where I'm weak."

#### How We Serve This Persona
- Targeted, personalized tutoring (diagnostic assessment first)
- Online session options with quality video conferencing
- Student portal with practice resources accessible 24/7
- Flexible scheduling with easy rescheduling process
- Progress dashboards showing improvement over time

## Persona 3: The Overwhelmed Student

### Maya Patel
**Age**: 15  
**Grade**: 9th (Freshman)  
**Location**: Suburban area  
**Tech Savviness**: Medium  

#### Background
- Struggling with the transition to high school
- Behind in Algebra 1, which is affecting confidence
- Quiet and hesitant to ask questions in class
- Parents noticed declining grades and sought help

#### Goals
- Build confidence in math fundamentals
- Catch up with classmates
- Develop better study habits and organization skills
- Feel less anxious about tests and homework

#### Pain Points
- Feels embarrassed about being behind
- Previous tutors made her feel "dumb" for not understanding quickly
- Easily discouraged when she doesn't see immediate progress
- Homework takes hours, leading to burnout

#### Behaviors
- Prefers one-on-one attention (not group tutoring)
- Responds well to patient, encouraging teaching
- Benefits from breaking down complex problems into small steps
- Needs frequent positive reinforcement

#### Quote
> "I just want to feel like I can do this. Everyone else seems to get it, and I'm left confused."

#### How We Serve This Persona
- Patient, empathetic tutoring approach (emphasized in marketing)
- Focus on building foundational skills, not just test prep
- Celebrate small wins and incremental progress
- Parent communication to reinforce positive changes
- Study skills coaching alongside subject tutoring

## Persona 4: The Budget-Conscious Family

### The Johnson Family
**Parents**: Marcus (38) and Lisa (36)  
**Children**: Two middle-schoolers (6th and 8th grade)  
**Location**: Suburban area  
**Household Income**: Moderate  

#### Background
- Both children need help with different subjects (one in math, one in English)
- Single-income household; budget is tight
- Value education but must be mindful of costs
- Looking for quality without overpaying

#### Goals
- Get affordable tutoring for both children
- Find package deals or multi-sibling discounts
- Ensure value: Are the sessions effective?
- Avoid hidden fees or surprise charges

#### Pain Points
- Many tutoring services are expensive ($80-100+/hour)
- Concerned about paying for ineffective tutoring
- Need to balance tutoring cost with other family expenses
- Don't want to feel "nickel and dimed"

#### Behaviors
- Compares pricing across multiple tutoring options
- Looks for discounts, packages, or promotional offers
- Reads reviews carefully to assess value
- May start with just a few sessions to test before committing

#### Quote
> "We want the best for our kids, but we can't afford to pay $100/hour. Is there something that works for families like ours?"

#### How We Serve This Persona
- Transparent, competitive pricing clearly displayed
- Multi-session packages with discounts
- Sibling discounts prominently featured
- Free initial consultation to build trust
- Flexible payment options (payment plans, if possible)

## Anti-Persona: Who We Don't Serve

### The "Quick Fix" Seeker
- Expects the tutor to "do the work" for their child
- Not interested in building long-term skills
- Focused solely on short-term grade manipulation
- Unrealistic expectations (e.g., "Can you guarantee an A?")

**Why**: Our focus is on sustainable learning and skill development, not shortcuts or academic dishonesty.

## How to Use These Personas

### In Design Decisions
- **Ask**: "Would Sarah be able to find pricing easily?" "Would Alex find the mobile experience frustrating?"

### In Feature Prioritization
- **Example**: Student portal benefits Alex and Maya more than Sarah (parents prefer email updates)

### In Marketing Copy
- **Example**: Emphasize "affordable packages" for The Johnsons, "expert SAT strategies" for Alex

### In User Testing
- **Recruit**: Participants matching these personas for usability tests
